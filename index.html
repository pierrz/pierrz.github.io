Crafting Data

With Experience

After work experience in Sound techniques & Photography, I progressively shifted into Data Engineering and Web Development while being involved in projects related to Digital Asset Management, Video on Demand and Digital Humanities.

I follow in my process the fundamentals of UX Design tempered with technical insights, in order to translate and implement the results into functional, attractive and scalable interfaces relying on solid Data Architecture and elegant Web Design.

I currently work as a Data Engineer at GeoPhy, a Dutch startup disrupting the real estate market by delivering clear, automated and instantanous property valuation, hence bringing transparency to this complex domain.

I am also developing a streamlined, data-driven platform for cultural institutions to improve their collections with Linked Data and IIIF standards. It will soon be launched at Muzz.app, and I'll be speaking about it at the 2019 IIIF Conference in Göttingen, Germany.

Want to know more about me ? Have a look on what I do & likewhat I do & like, and here is my resume.

Based on User Research/Design tecniques, I take the pulse of what is at stakes and challenge it : requirements VS hidden needs, potential users VS real ones, resources VS strategy, data & information VS content corpus.

Once the core elements are well identified, they can be translated into a tangible Information Architecture in order to emphasize, merge or discard them. While staying on a conceptual level, it remains the foundations for a specific Data Architecture strategy afterwards.

With this overall approach, digital interfaces can be designed with a very minimal and dynamic code relying on Data-Driven Design principles. Then, it is just about finding the right balance between ergonomy and aesthetics, simplicity and control ... just joking. It's mostly about putting a lot of stuff in a very limited space.
