After work experience in Sound techniques and Photography, I progressively dived into the realm of digital products and graduated with a Master of Information Architecture from the Ecole Nationale Supérieure de Lyon in France.

I got different titles such as UX Designer and Project Manager until I progressively trained myself in Data engineering and Solution architecture throughout projects both in the private and non-profit sectors.
I am now looking to take on positions with more responsibilities such as Product Manager.

I have had the chance to be involved in a variety of domains such as digital asset management, video on demand, digital humanities, banking, real estate, digital marketplace, aviation and food security monitoring.

Guided by my initial training in Information Architecture and my work experience with Agile/SCRUM techniques, I tend to follow design principles while taking great care of gathering and considering every technical aspects with all due respect.
My drive is to properly define and structure the work at hand and eventually build up momentum as each component gets delivered, both to save time/resources and to use it as a stepping-stone for further developments, whichever it could be.

I currently work at the World Food Program in Rome as a Software engineer, refactoring the data pipelines which support the data collection related to food security monitoring. Part of this work can be seen through the HungerMap product, a live dashboard focused on the situation of 30+ countries.

Besides, I have designed and developed a streamlined, data-driven platform for cultural institutions to improve their collections with Linked Data and IIIF standards, and embedded with Computer vision features. I presented this project at the 2019 IIIF Conference in Göttingen, Germany. It is available at Muzai.io.
