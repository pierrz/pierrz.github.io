keyboard_arrow_down

Based on User Research/Design tecniques, I take the pulse of what is at stakes and challenge it : requirements VS hidden needs, potential users VS real ones, resources VS strategy, data & information VS content corpus.

Once the core elements are well identified, they can be translated into a tangible Information Architecture in order to emphasize, merge or discard them. While staying on a conceptual level, it remains the foundations for a specific Data Architecture strategy afterwards.

With this overall approach, digital interfaces can be designed with a very minimal and dynamic code relying on Data-Driven Design principles. Then, it is just about finding the right balance between ergonomy and aesthetics, simplicity and control ... just joking. It's mostly about putting a lot of stuff in a very limited space.
